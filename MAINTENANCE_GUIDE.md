# JNAS Returnable Asset Maintenance & Repair Manual

This manual details the standard preventative and restorative procedures for returnable asset components.

## Categories of Work

### 1. Preventative Maintenance (Routine)
- **Interval**: Every 50 cycles or 6 months.
- **Actions**:
  - Tighten high-tensile joint bolts to design torque specs (e.g., 45 Nm).
  - Lubricate heavy caster wheel bearings with synthetic lithium grease.
  - Wash down polyurethane padding to remove abrasive steel shards.

### 2. Repair (Ad-hoc)
- **Interval**: On failed inspection or structural wear trigger.
- **Actions**:
  - Spot weld micro-fractures in structural columns.
  - Replace damaged thermoformed plastic divider sheets.
  - Re-align distorted guide pins.

### 3. Refurbishment (Major Overhaul)
- **Interval**: On reaching 80% maximum cycle lifetime or extreme collision damage.
- **Actions**:
  - Complete bead blasting and powder coating of steel framing.
  - Full replacement of protective lining kits.
  - Recalibrate sensor nodes and update BLE battery pack modules.

---

## Part Replacement Logs

Every maintenance log must specify:
1. **Replacement Parts Used**: e.g., `['Bearing Cast-X1', 'Poly-Spacer Block']`.
2. **Recorded Labor and Costs**: Tracking the financial durability footprint of reusable designs compared to single-use wood alternatives.
3. **Audit Revision Trails**: Ensuring work was completed by a certified Workshop Technician before returning the asset back to the active fleet.
