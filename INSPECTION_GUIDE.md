# JNAS Quality Inspection Protocol & Safety Guide

Quality checks are mandatory for reusable assets to ensure transit durability and prevent damage to expensive component payloads.

## Inspection Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

### 1. Structural Check
- Inspect all primary structural members for buckling, micro-cracking, or weld failure.
- Ensure locking mechanisms, hinges, and caster pins are functional.

### 2. Envelope & Pad Review
- Check customized foam linings, rubber inserts, and protective bumpers.
- Ensure no delamination or contamination with corrosive fluids has occurred.

### 3. Tracking Verification
- Visually inspect QR labels or RFID tag enclosures.
- Use a test reader to verify signal resonance.

---

## Technical Log Structure

When logging inspection results, inspectors must assign a precise condition and damage level:

- **Condition Classifications**:
  - `Excellent`: New or fully restored asset with zero wear.
  - `Good`: Standard operational wear; structural integrity intact.
  - `Fair`: Minor surface scratch; safe to operate.
  - `Poor`: Localized buckling or fatigue; must be sent to Maintenance.
  - `Damaged`: Structural failure; immediate quarantine required.

- **Damage Scale**:
  - `None`: Ready for load-staging.
  - `Cosmetic`: minor scrapes, decals peeling.
  - `Structural Minor`: Buckled side brackets; needs workshop review.
  - `Structural Major`: Severely warped beams; locked for overhaul.

- **Action Recommendations**:
  - `Pass`: Approved for active service.
  - `Monitor`: Minor wear; inspect again in 30 days.
  - `Repair Required`: Send to maintenance workshop.
  - `Decommission`: Scrapped.
